Obtaining Terciles

To obtain a histogram of the tercile probabilities for precipitation and temperature, move your cursor over any land area location on the world map and click.


You may ZOOM in on a desired region of the world by changing the latitude and longitude settings of the display. LATITUDE settings are located to the left of the world map, LONGITUDE settings are located below the map. To specify a new latitude range, edit the values in the boxes to the left of the map. Use "N" to indicate degrees north, "S" to indicate degrees south. Similary, to specify new longitude settings, edit the values in the boxes below the plot. Use "W" to indicate degrees west and "E" to indicate degees east.

The forecast is expressed in terms of the probabilities of each of three categories, above­normal, is defined to be within the wettest third of historically recorded precipitation total or the warmest third of historically recorded mean temperature for a given region; below­normal is defined to be the driest third of precipitation total or the coldest third of mean temperature; normal, is the third centered on the climatological median.


It is anticipated that each rectangular region identified will have distinct interior climate variability, especially in area having terrain features or near large bodies of water. The forecast should be interpreted as a mean condition over the entire region.


The Climatology Plots


The monthly climatologies of precipitation and temperature for a selected point are displayed in mm/day. Horizontal lines are drawn at 1mm/day intervals. The monthly climatologies of temperature are also displayed. The mean temperature is plotted with a solid line, the range is indicated by bars, where the top (bottom) of each bar indicates the average maximum (minimum)temperature.Horizontal lines are drawn at intervals of 5 degrees C.

Select the Redraw button (to the lower-left of map) to hide HELP
