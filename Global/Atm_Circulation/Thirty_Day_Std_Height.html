30-Day Average Standardized Geopotential Height Anomaly

PAST 30 DAYS 250 hPa

PAST 30 DAYS 500 hPa

PAST 30 DAYS 925 hPa

Data:  NCEP reanalysis daily geopotential height (250, 500, and 925 hPa) on a 2.5 x 2.5 deg. lat/lon grid.
Data Source:  NCEP

Description:
The maps above show 30-day average standardized geopotential height anomalies with respect to the 1971-2000 climatology (grey contours) at 250 (top), 500 (middle), and 925 hPa (bottom) for the last 30 days. The shading for the anomalies starts at +/-1 standard deviation; the color contour interval is 0.5 s.d. Positive anomalies are shown in yellow and orange; negative anomalies in shades of blue. The climatology contour interval in the 250 and 500 hPa maps is 60 gpm, and is 30 gpm in the 925 hPa map.   NCEP is an acronym for the National Centers for Environmental Prediction, US.
