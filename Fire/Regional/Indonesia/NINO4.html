Exploratory Predictive Tool for Fire Activity in Indonesia

The empirical relationship between number of fires and the NINO4 index based on 9 years of data allow us to create an exploratory tool for forecasting the likelihood of fire activity.

Monthly NINO4 Index, November 1998 to Present

Access to Data

Predicting Fire Activity 1 Month in Advance

Predicting Fire Activity 1 Month in Advance:


Use the value of the NINO4 index in April, May, June, July, August, or September on the X axis of the graphs on the left to determine for each province the risk of fire activity for the following month as indicated by recent relationships found between NINO4 and number of fires (data are based on time series between 1998 and 2006).
The graphs show the relationship between number of fires and the NINO4 index in the previous month for each province in Kalimantan. A positive NINO4 index value indicates a higher probability of fire occurrence. The contingency table and dashed lines are based on the median values both for NINO4 and the number of fires.

Predicting Fire Activity 2 Months in Advance

Predicting Fire Activity 2 Months in Advance:


Use the value of the NINO4 index in April, May, June, July, August, or September on the X axis of the graphs on the left to determine for each province the risk of fire activity for two months later as indicated by recent relationships found between NINO4 and number of fires (data are based on time series between 1998 and 2006).
The graphs show the relationship between number of fires and the NINO4 index two months prior for each province in Kalimantan. A positive NINO4 index value indicates a higher probability of fire occurrence. The contingency table and dashed lines are based on the median values both for NINO4 and the number of fires.


Data:  Monthly NINO4 index based upon Reynolds and Smith OIv2 sea surface temperature anomalies
Data Period:  November 1998 - present
Base Period for Climatology:  January 1971 - December 2000
Data Source:  NCEP Environmental Modeling Center

Description:   The time series at the top of the page gives the value of the NINO4 sea surface temperature anomaly index in the central equatorial Pacific Ocean (description of NINO4 index) based upon sea surface temperature anomalies in the Reynolds and Smith OIv2 data set (description), with 1971-2000 as the climatological base period.   NCEP is an acronym for the US National Centers for Environmental Prediction.
