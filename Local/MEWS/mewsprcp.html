Description
a) Dekadal (i.e., ~10-daily) precipitation estimates for the selected region from Dec 1999 to the present.

b) Same as (a) (solid black line) with the addition of the climatological precipitation for the same region (grey dotted line). The blue (red) bars are indicative of above-normal (below-normal) precipitation, based on climatology.

c) Same as (a) for the current year (thick black line), as indicated by the axis labels. Precipitation estimates from previous years are also shown (blue-1 yr from present; magenta- 2 yrs from present; grey-3 yrs from present).

d) Cumulative dekadal satellite-derived precipitation estimates (solid black line) and climatological cumulative precipitation (grey dotted line) for the most recent 12-month period in the selected region. The blue (red) bars are indicative of above-normal (below-normal) cumulative precipitation, based on climatology.

NOTE: The dekadal precipitation estimates and climatological precipitation data are produced with different methods and data sources. Arthimetic comparisons between the two data series are therefore not discrete quantities. They may, however, provide working information on changes in malaria risk in areas where precipitation anomalies are the principal cause of malaria epidemics.
