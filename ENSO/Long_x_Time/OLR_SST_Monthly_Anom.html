Monthly OLR and SST Anomaly

This is a Hovmoller diagram (Longitude x Time) showing monthly outgoing longwave radiation (OLR) anomalies and sea surface temperature (SST) anomalies averaged over 5°S to 5°N, for the past 36 months across the equatorial Pacific Ocean.

The green and brown shading indicate the monthly OLR anomaly from the 1979-present climatology. Negative anomalies (in shades of green) imply increased cloudiness and an enhanced likelihood that there is precipitation. Positive anomalies (in shades of brown) imply decreased cloudiness and precipitation. The shading start at +/- 5 W/m2, with a shading interval of 10 W/m2. The relationship between OLR anomaly and precipitation is most reliable in tropical regions. Red (blue) contours indicate monthly SST anomalies above (below) the 1971-2000 mean; the contour interval is 0.5°C. The longitude domain extends from 120°E to 80°W. On this chart the last 36 months is shown, and time increases vertically.

Dataset Documentation

Monthly OLR Anomaly Data

Data
Monthly OLR anomaly values from NOAA AVHRR observations on a 2.5° lat/lon grid, between 5°S and 5°N
Data Source
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) Climate Prediction Center (CPC) (Outgoing Longwave Radiation)
Analysis
Equatorial average of monthly-average outgoing longwave radiation anomalies (1979-1995 base period), displayed as a longitude x time Hovmoller diagram

Monthly SST Anomaly Data

Data
Reynolds and Smith OISST Version 2 monthly sea surface temperature anomaly (in °C) with respect to the 1971-2000 base period on a 1.0° lat/lon grid, between 5°S and 5°N
Data Source
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) (OISST Version 2)
Analysis
Equatorial average of monthly sea surface temperature anomalies with respect to the 1971-2000 monthly climatology displayed as a longitude x time Hovmoller diagram
