Seasonal OLR and Standardized Streamfunction Anomalies
Seasonal OLR Anomaly (W/m2) and 200 hPa Standardized Streamfunction Anomaly

Seasonal OLR Anomaly (W/m2) and 925 hPa Standardized Streamfunction Anomaly

Outgoing Longwave Radiation Data:   NCEP/CPC Monthly Outgoing Longwave Radiation ("OLR") on a 2.5° x 2.5° lat/lon grid
Base Period for OLR Climatology:  Jan 1979-present
Streamfunction Data: NCEP/NCAR Reanalysis Streamfunction on a 2.5° x 2.5° lat/lon grid
Base Period for Streamfunction Climatology: Jan 1971-Dec 2000
Data Source: NCEP

Description

Top:  Seasonal (3-month) average OLR anomaly (in Watts/m2) from the long-term monthly mean (green and brown shading at 10 W/m2 interval) and seasonal average 200 hPa standardized streamfunction anomaly (blue contours at 0.5 s.d. interval) over the tropical Indian and Pacific Ocean basins. Negative OLR anomaly values imply increased cloudiness and enhanced likelihood of precipitation.  Positive anomalies imply decreased cloudiness/precipitation.  These relationships work best in tropical regions.

Bottom:  Same as top plot, but with streamfunction at 925 hPa
