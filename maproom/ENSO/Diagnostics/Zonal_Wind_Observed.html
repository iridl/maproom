925 hPa Pentad Observed Zonal Wind

This is a Hovmoller diagram (Longitude x Time) showing pentad (five-day) average zonal winds at 925 hPa averaged over 5°S to 5°N, for the past year across the equatorial Pacific Ocean.

Contours are drawn at intervals of 2 m/s; negative values indicate easterlies, and positive values represent westerlies. The longitude domain extends from 120°E to 90°W, and data from the last year are shown, with time increasing vertically.

Dataset Documentation

925 hPa Pentad Observed Zonal Wind

Data
NCEP-NCAR Reanalysis daily zonal wind at 925 hPa (in m/s) on a 2.5° lat/lon grid
Data Source
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) (NCEP/NCAR Reanalysis)
Analysis
Equatorial average of pentad-average zonal wind, displayed as a longitude x time Hovmoller diagram
