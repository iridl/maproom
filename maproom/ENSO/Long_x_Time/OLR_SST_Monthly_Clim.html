Monthly OLR and SST Climatology

This is a Hovmoller diagram (Longitude x Time) showing the outgoing longwave radiation (OLR) monthly climatology and sea surface temperature (SST) monthly climatology averaged over 5°S to 5°N across the equatorial Pacific Ocean.

The green and grey shading represent values of the monthly OLR climatology over the 1979-present base period. Green shading indicates areas where the monthly climatological OLR is less than 240 W/m2, a general indicator of precipitation in the tropics. Black contours represent monthly climatological SST values over the 1971-2000 base period; the contour interval is 1.0°C. The longitude domain extends from 120°E to 80°W. The vertical axis represents the months of the calendar year, from January at the bottom to December at the top.

Dataset Documentation

Monthly OLR Climatology Data

Data
Monthly OLR values from NOAA AVHRR observations on a 2.5° lat/lon grid, between 5°S and 5°N
Data Source
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) Climate Prediction Center (CPC) (Outgoing Longwave Radiation)
Analysis
Equatorial average of monthly climatological outgoing longwave radiation (1979-present base period), displayed as a longitude x time Hovmoller diagram

Monthly SST Climatology Data

Data
Reynolds and Smith OISST Version 2 sea surface temperature monthly climatology (in °C) over the 1971-2000 base period on a 1.0° lat/lon grid, between 5°S and 5°N
Data Source
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Prediction (NCEP) (OISST Version 2)
Analysis
Equatorial average of monthly climatological sea surface temperature (1971-2000 base period) displayed as a longitude x time Hovmoller diagram
