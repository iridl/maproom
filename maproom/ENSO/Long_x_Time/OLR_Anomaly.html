Pentad Outgoing Longwave Radiation Anomaly

This is a Hovmoller diagram (Longitude x Time) showing five-day average outgoing longwave radiation (OLR) anomalies averaged over 5°S to 5°N, for the past year across the equatorial Pacific Ocean.

The OLR anomalies are contoured at an interval of 20 W/m2, and the contours and shading start at +/- 10 W/m2. Negative anomalies (in shades of green) imply increased cloudiness and an enhanced likelihood that there is precipitation. Positive anomalies (in shades of brown) imply decreased cloudiness and precipitation. The relationship between OLR anomaly and precipitation is most reliable in tropical regions. The base period used to calculate the anomalies is 1979-1995. The longitude domain extends from 120°E to 70°W. On this chart the last year is shown, and time increases vertically.
