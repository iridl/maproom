Obtaining Climatologies

To obtain a local climatogy of precipitation and temperature, simply move your cursor over any land area location on the world map and click. NOTE: No data is available over water or Antarctica.


You may ZOOM in on a desired region of the world by changing the latitude and longitude settings of the display. LATITUDE settings are located to the left of the world map, LONGITUDE settings are located below the map. To specify a new latitude range, edit the values in the boxes to the left of the map. Use "N" to indicate degrees north, "S" to indicate degrees south. Similarly, to specify new longitude settings, edit the values in the boxes below the plot. Use "W" to indicate degrees west and "E" to indicate degrees east.


The Climatology Plots

The base period for all of these plots is 1961 - 1990.
The data is in gridded format on a 0.5 by 0.5 degree grid.

The monthly climatologies of precipitation and temperature for a selected point are displayed in four plots:

  • Average monthly Precipitation is displayed in the upper-left plot in mm/day. Horizontal lines are drawn at 1mm/day intervals.
  • The Wet-Day Frequency is plotted directly below the monthly precipitation. This indicates the average number of days in a given month with observed precipitation. Horizontal lines are drawn at intervals of 5 days.
  • Average Temperature is displayed in the upper-right plot. The average diurnal (daily) range in temperature is also indicated. The mean temperature is plotted with a solid line, the diurnal range is indicated by bars; the top (bottom) of each bar indicates the average maximum (minimum) temperature. Horizontal lines are drawn at intervals of 5 degrees C.
  • A Ground-Frost Frequency plot is displayed directly below the mean temperature. This plot indicates the average number of days in a given month where the minimum temperature is below 0 degrees C. Horizontal lines are drawn at intervals of 5 days.

Select the Redraw button (to the lower-left of map) to hide HELP
